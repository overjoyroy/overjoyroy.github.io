Hi! I'm Joy. I hold a PhD in Biomedical Informatics from the University of Pittsburgh School of Medicine. Previously, I worked as a full-time Software Engineer in the telecommunication and defense sector. My research focuses on the intersection of data science and software engineering, particularly within clinical applications. I am passionate about applying a systems-based approach to medicine and developing novel methods for computer-aided detection and predictive modeling.

In my doctoral research, I leveraged machine learning, graph theory, and neuroimaging datasets to build explainable models that enhance our understanding of neurodevelopment. These models are designed to enable intuitive inferences for clinical cases, offering insights for both typical and atypical patient populations. Moving forward, I am committed to advancing data-driven approaches to address complex biomedical challenges.
