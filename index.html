About

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Biomedical Informatics at the University of Pittsburgh School of Medicine. Prior to this, I worked as a full-time Software Engineer in the telecommunication and defense sector. My research interests lie at the intersection of data science and software engineering, specifically within healthcare applications. I am fascinated by the systems approach to studying medicine and am driven to develop novel methods that facilitate computer-aided detection and predictive models.

Currently, I am exploring the use of machine learning, graph theory, and neuroimaging datasets to create explainable models that can deepen our understanding of neurodevelopment. My aim is to use these models to make intuitive inferences on clinical cases for both typical and non-typical patients.
